Origin of the Term "Hot Dog"

History

While sausages had been around for quite some time, the actual name "hot dog" was coined by cartoonist Thomas "Tad" Dorgan. Dorgan was drawing a cartoon of a "sausage in a roll" for Harry Stevens' concession stand. Dorgan couldn't spell "dachshund," so instead he wrote "hot dog." The name stuck and is used to this day.
